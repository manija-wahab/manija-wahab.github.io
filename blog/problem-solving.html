Problem Solving

By Noorya Wahab

a time I was blocked on a simple problem

So, the problem was with this basic JavaScript task where I needed to create a loop to iterate over an array and perform a task. It seemed simple at first, but I just couldn't figure out why my loop wasn't working properly. I tried a few problem-solving techniques. First, I went back to the basics of Javascript and reviewed my understanding of loops. Then, I tried breaking down the problem into smaller steps to see if I missed something. I also tried looking at examples online to see if there was a similar problem someone else had encountered. Throughout the process, I started to doubt myself and ability as I kept thinking, "Why can't I figure this out? It's so easy!!!" But I tried to stay patient and give it one more shot but this time I decided to plan everything out first and it all worked out so I learnt how critical planning is in coding.

A time I elegantly solved a problem

When I was working on creating my blog, I wanted to add glowing circles to the background. But making sure they looked good on all screen sizes was a little tough. Instead of rushing and being reckless, I knew I needed to plan carefully. So, I sat down with a piece of paper and drew out how I wanted my blog to look. I thought about how it would appear on smaller devices as well such as phones. This planning really helped when I started coding. With a clear idea of what to do, things went smoothly. I could handle any problems that popped up because I had thought about them beforehand. It made the whole process much easier and I elegantly solved my issues.

how confident I feel using problem-solving techniques / processes

I feel quite confident using pseudocode as planning beforehand is really useful and makes your work a lot easier while the Rubber ducky method is something I haven't tried much, but I think it could be helpful in clarifying my thoughts through explaining the problem to someone or something. Reading error messages has always been great help for when i'm trying to troubleshoot my code but console.logging is like a hero for me when things get too confusing and I think I would struggle a lot if it didn’t exist, just seeing what my code looks like at different points is so useful. Google can be useful for simple problems but its not that useful for when things get too complex. I don’t enjoy asking my peers/coaches for help because i’d rather try to solve it on my own and learn using my own brain but if things get too tough I feel confident enough to reach out for help. Reflecting feels nice to go back and see where you went wrong and what you can improve on next time.

A time I was reluctant to ask for help

There was this one time when I was stuck on a question for a really long time and I couldn't solve it on my own. I kept struggling with it for hours because I refused to be defeated by that question and I wanted to solve it on my own no matter what. I also felt like my question was a bit silly and too easy so I was afraid to ask it. However, looking back, I realized that it would’ve been better if I had just asked for help as it would’ve saved me a lot of time and I've also come to understand that even if your question is silly or simple, it's better to ask anyways as it shows a willingness to learn and collaborate with others and someone else might also be stuck on the same thing and you asking for help could help them out too.
