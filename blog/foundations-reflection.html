Foundations
Reflection

Reflecting on my learning journey and all of the Core Skills I learnt in the Foundations curriculum

By Noorya Wahab

Question One
What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

> I realized that at the start of each challenge I tend to have a high ego thinking it's easy and achievable which I think is a good thing because it makes me believe that I can do it. However this causes me to act recklessly without planning and that never ends too well and once I start to see multiple failures my ego drops down a lot and I start to get a lot of self doubts however once I complete the challenge I feel a strong sense of gratification and joy and I realize that I truly can achieve anything as long as I believe myself.

> My ego also tends to hold me back from asking for help, I realized just how much I hesitate to ask for help and would rather struggle all on my own than ask for help I feel like my ego wants me to push myself and solve my own problems but when I ask for help I don't feel the same gratification that I do when I try to complete a task all by myself.

> Overall, the core learning journey has not only deepened my understanding of programming but has also shown me some profound self-reflection, showing me just how powerful self-awareness can be.

Question Two
What are the roles of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

> Values, empathy, and self-awareness are really important when working with other programmers. First, values. They're like our guide, helping us make fair and honest decisions when we work together. Whether it's about respecting each other's ideas or being honest about how we're doing, sticking to values like honesty and teamwork helps us build trust and get along well with our team and understand their ideas and point of view.

> Empathy is also just as important, it's about understanding how our teammates feel and what they need. When we can see things from their point of view, we can work together better, share ideas, and support each other when things get hard. It's like being a good friend: you listen, you understand, and you're there for each other.

> And when things get too stressful for the people you're working with you need to be there for them and empathize with them to help them and when you're in the same situation you would have that bond where they can do the same for you. Lastly, self awareness is about knowing our own strengths and weaknesses and how our minds work. By being self aware, we can communicate better, take on tasks that we're good at and you can see your true potential to grow and improve.

Question Three
What has surprised you the most about core learning?

> Learning all about neuroplasticity and growth mindset during the core learning was truly surprising for me and how people can learn and grow at any age and there is no limitation to learning if someone truly wants to learn they can do itttt

> Additionally, I've been amazed by just how much self-growth I've experienced throughout the process. It's been surprising to see how much I've grown not just as a programmer, but as a person, thanks to the emphasis on values, empathy, and self-awareness. I honestly didn't expect a coding bootcamp like Dev Academy to place such importance on topics like mental health and personal development, but it's been incredibly rewarding to see how much they care about the students wellbeing.

> The emphasis on values, empathy, and self-awareness has been truly impactful, and it's heartening to know that they prioritize not just technical skills, but also holistic growth and support for their students.

Question Four
What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

> Delving deeper into your own mind and understanding yourself better was definitely one of the toughest parts of the core learning journey. Taking a step back now and then to reflect on the patterns of your thoughts and how your mind operates was challenging. It's easy to overlook these small details and forget to pay attention to them while you're busy coding something or stuck on a problem.

> When something goes wrong in your code, it's hard to pause and consider how you'll respond and notice similar patterns in your learning journey. It's like asking yourself, "Am I doubting myself? Do I feel like giving up or pushing forward? Do I have a growth mindset?" It takes a lot of patience, but it's definitely worth it in the end.

> Also finding time every morning to meditate can also be a challenge, especially for those who may live in loud environments or have busy mornings. It's tough to make time for these important practices and plan your day around them but making space for these moments of self reflection and mindfulness is crucial for personal growth and well-being.

Question Five
Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

> I think Dev Academy focuses a lot on core learning because it can get really stressful and in moments like these it's really important to have those skills that can help you bounce right back when things go wrong or else an individual that lacks emotional intelligence or skills that are taught in core, might end up in a stressful situation and then they won't be able to cope.

> In the world of programming, stress and challenges are unavoidable. That's why it's crucial for us to develop not only technical skills but also emotional intelligence and resilience. When things go wrong in your code or you're under a lot of pressure, staying calm and finding solutions is key to thrive in the tech industry.

> It's not just about coding, it's about working well with others too. Whether we're working on group projects or talking to clients, having the right mindset is so important as a programmer

Question Six
Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time?

> To me, core learning wasn't a waste of time at all. It's like laying down the foundation for a sturdy building. Without it, the structure would crumble at the slightest challenge. Core learning has taught me things that programming alone couldn't, like understanding my own emotions better and learning how to navigate through stressful situations with a calm mind.

> Sure, I could have spent all that time coding, but without the emotional intelligence and self-awareness gained from core learning, I might have struggled more when things got tough. This whole concept of core learning is so well done in Dev Academy because I've seen just how much studies can affect the mental health of students in different universities. I've seen many students break down and go into depression, and I think it's incredible how much emphasis is put on the mental health of students in Dev Academy because students are humans too.

> Some people can easily be affected negatively when faced with challenges, and core learning just teaches students to spend more time understanding themselves, improving their emotional intelligence, and their resilience when learning, and what it means to be a programmer and think like one.
