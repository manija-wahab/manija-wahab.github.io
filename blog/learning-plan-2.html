Plan 2

How I will operate within the context of Foundations and Bootcamp at Dev Academy

By Noorya Wahab

Question One
What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

> Once I leave dev academy, I hope to join the tech world and simply create. I don’t exactly know what I want to create, but I know I'll make something meaningful to me and others.

> If possible, I would also like to fulfill my dream of becoming an indie game developer. But for now, my focus is solely on entering the tech field, expanding my knowledge over the years, and creating many things with my skills.

> For example, I used to spend hours searching for a good epub reading app so I could easily read my books with all the desired features. However, I was never able to find one. So maybe when I become a software developer, I can create my own app, which might be useful for someone else like me!!

Question Two
What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

> My biggest strength will definitely be my time management. Bootcamp will be busier, and I tend to complete work really fast and efficiently because I work at my own pace and prefer deep focus on a task, completing it once, rather than breaking it into many segments over days.

> I usually completed most of my foundations work in the first week, then spent the next week learning things outside of devacademy to further improve. My biggest limitation would be working with people because I'm not the best at communicating.

> I have a lot of social anxiety and prefer working independently because I feel like I have control over everything. But I'm afraid of letting down the people I'm working with or being the reason they struggle, so that might be a limitation.

Question Three
What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

> Again, the biggest challenge for me would be working with people and communicating.

> I'm really bad at that, and I feel a lot of anxiety, so I usually prefer to work independently. But I know that as a software developer, where you work with many people, that's a critical skill you need to have.

> So I think it's important that I go through this to get out of my comfort zone and face my fears. But working in groups still feels a little scary and challenging for me.

Question Four
What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

> Communication is definitely something that I want to improve and get better at.

> I want to be able to speak confidently and learn what it feels like to work with others because it's really important for me to get out of my comfort zone, or else I won't grow.

> I would also like to develop other human skills such as working with others, managing being in a group, working on projects with multiple people, and maybe even being a leader.

Question Five
WWhat are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

> I hope that the Bootcamp team understands when situations get a little tough for us students, such as if an event occurs where we are unable to attend a meeting, etc.

> I hope they can understand and give us time. I’m also hoping that they understand my social anxiety and won't pressure me too much when it comes to communicating with others.

> I want to grow at my own pace and maybe even take baby steps because I know that too much pressure will do the opposite and cause my fears to grow.

Question Six
What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

> I am hoping that I will do as well as I did in foundations during Bootcamp, and I will complete my work on time with quality.

> When it comes to working with others, I hope I will be able to be a good member of the group, contribute, and help as much as I can. I am also hoping that I will not hesitate to ask for help whenever I really need it instead of trying to stress myself out just to complete it myself.

> I will set a time limit for myself when I struggle on a particular task and if I don't find a solution in that set time then I will immediately go ask for help.
