What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Think of your web page like a neighborhood with houses representing different elements like headings or paragraphs. The spaces around these houses are similar to the concepts of margins, borders, and padding.

Margins are like the roads:

Just as roads separate houses in a neighborhood, margins separate elements on our page. Imagine each element as one of those orange houses, and the margin is the space/road between them, providing a clear separation between each of the houses.

The white space around the green headings is because there is a margin set which seperates the headings from the parapgrahs like this margin: 10px;

Paddings are like the green lawns:

Look at the green lawns around each house. This is similar to padding. When we give an element padding, it's like adding extra space around the house inside the margin which creates a comfortable buffer zone between the houses and the road.

Notice how around the headings there is a light green space around the text? Well thats because there is a padding set on each of the headings like this padding: 2%;

Borders are like the white fences:

Visualize the white fences around the houses. These fences act like borders. The border is the boundary defining the edges of an element and its padding. It gives a nice outline to our element and a distinct appearance.

Now do you notice the darker green outline around the headings padding, well thats the border that I have set for them like this border: solid rgb(56, 105, 54) 5px;

In summary, just as roads, lawns, and fences shape the space around houses in a neighborhood, margins, padding, and borders help us design and structure the space around elements on our webpage.
